Elton John

The Rocket Man

Here is a timeline of Elton Johns's amazing life

  • 1947 - Elton John was born Reginald Kenneth Dwight to Sheila Eileen and Stanley Dwight in Pinner, London.
  • 1950 - Reginald began playing the family piano.
  • 1958 - Reginald won a junior scholarship to the Royal Academy of music and attended classes every Saturday.
  • 1962 - Reginald Dwight took an evening weekend job playing the piano at the Northwood Hills Hotel. He was billed as Reggie and played and sang popular favourite songs as well as his own compositions.
  • 1962 - Reggie formed the band Bluesology.
  • 1965 - Bluesology secured billing as backing group for American bands touring the UK.
  • 1967 - After answering an advertisement in New Musical Express placed by Liberty Records, Reggie met Bernie Taupin, a lyricist, beginning a lifelong song writing partnership.
  • 1968 - Reginald Dwight began using the name Elton John and was producing music with Bernie Taupin. Elton and Bernie joined DJM Records as songwriters.
  • 1968 - Elton released his first single “I’ve Been Loving You”.
  • 1953 - Elton released his first album “Empty Sky”.
  • 1962 - Elton John released a second album “Elton John”. It reached number 5 in the UK and number 4 in the US charts.
  • 1972 - Reginald Dwight legally changed his name to Elton Hercules John by deed poll.
  • 1972 - The single “Rocket Man” (My favourite) was released. It reached number 2 in the UK and number 8 in the US.
  • 1973 - Together with Bernie Taupin and producer Gus Dudgeon, Elton founded the Rocket Record Company.
  • 1975 - Elton overdosed on cocaine and developed bulimia due to pressure of work.
  • 1976 - Elton John became Chairman and Managing Director of Watford Football Club.
  • 1976 - Elton John announced that he was bisexual in an interview with Rolling Stone Magazine.
  • 1979 - Elton John became the first western artist to appear live in the Soviet Union. He played at Moscow and St Petersburg.
  • 1983 - Elton John performed at the Sun City venue in South Africa going against the UN cultural boycott on South Africa over apartheid.
  • 1984 - Elton John married his close friend, Renate Blauel, a sound engineer, in Sydney, Australia.
  • 1984 - Elton John performed at the Live Aid charity concert organised by Bob Geldof and Midge Ure.
  • 1984 - Elton John, Gladys Knight, Dionne Warwick, Stevie Wonder, Burt Bacharach and Carole Bayer-Sager recorded “That’s What Friends Are For” to raise funds for AIDS research. It reached number 16 in the UK and number 1 in the US.
  • 1987 - Elton John successfully sued The Sun newspaper for publishing false allegations of him paying for rent boys.
  • 1991 - Elton John was awarded Best British Male at the Brit Awards.
  • 1992 - Elton established the Elton John AIDS Foundation in the United States to raise money for HIV and AIDS prevention in the Americas and the Caribbean.
  • 1993 - Elton began a relationship with David Furnish, a Canadian filmmaker.
  • 1995 - Elton John was made Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE) for his charitable work.
  • 1997 - The single “Something About The Way You Look Tonight” / “Candle in the Wind” was released as a tribute towards Diana, Princess of Wales. It reached number 1 in the UK and number 1 in the US and sold 33 million copies worldwide.
  • 1998 - Elton John was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II.
  • 1999 - Elton was presented with the Grammy Legend Award.
  • 2007 - Elton John appeared at the Concert for Diana at Wembley Stadium organised by Prince William and Prince Harry.
  • 2010 - A son Zachary Jackson Levon Furnish-John was born to a surrogate mother in California USA.
  • 2012 - Elton performed at Queen Elizabeth II’s Diamond Jubilee Concert at Buckingham Palace.
  • 2013 - A son Elijah Joseph Daniel Furnish-John was born to the same surrogate mother as his brother in California USA.
  • 2014 - Elton, David and their sons attended Watford Football Club for the opening of the ‘Sir Elton John Stand’ at the ground.
  • 2014 - Elton John married his long-time partner David Furnish in Windsor, Berkshire.
  • 2019 - Elton John’s autobiography ‘Me’ was published.
